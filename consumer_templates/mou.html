MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING

Medical Transport Partnership Agreement

Between SkyCareLink Corp. and Authorized Transport Providers

Effective Date: August 1, 2025

1. PARTIES TO THE AGREEMENT

This Memorandum of Understanding ("MOU") is entered into between SkyCareLink Corp., a Delaware corporation with principal offices located at 123 Healthcare Plaza, Miami, FL 33101 ("SkyCareLink"), and the authorized medical transport provider ("Provider") as identified in the signature section below.

2. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

The purpose of this MOU is to establish a collaborative partnership for the provision of air medical transport services to patients and families in need of critical and non-critical medical transportation. This agreement defines the operational framework, revenue sharing, quality standards, and mutual obligations of both parties.

3. REVENUE SHARING AND COMPENSATION

3.1 Revenue Structure:

  • Standard Providers: 85% of booking value retained by Provider, 15% to SkyCareLink
  • Priority Partners: 90% of booking value retained by Provider, 10% to SkyCareLink
  • Diamond Partners: 92% of booking value retained by Provider, 8% to SkyCareLink

3.2 Additional Fees:

  • $1,000 non-refundable deposit collected by SkyCareLink per booking
  • Equipment fees (Ventilator +$5K, ECMO +$10K, etc.) passed through to Provider
  • Same-day urgency fees (20% upcharge) shared 50/50 between parties

3.3 Payment Terms:

SkyCareLink will remit Provider's share within 48 hours of confirmed transport completion. All payments will be made via ACH transfer to Provider's designated bank account.

4. PRIORITY PARTNER BENEFITS

Providers may qualify for Priority Partner status by meeting the following criteria:

  • Maintain 4.5+ star average customer rating
  • Complete 95%+ of accepted bookings
  • Respond to booking requests within 30 minutes
  • Maintain all required certifications and insurance

Priority Partner Benefits include:

  • Front and center placement in search results
  • Animated priority highlighting with gold border
  • Improved revenue share (90% vs 85%)
  • 24/7 priority support line
  • Quarterly performance bonuses
5. QUALITY STANDARDS AND COMPLIANCE

5.1 Medical Standards:

  • All aircraft must maintain current medical certification
  • Medical teams must be appropriately certified for transport severity levels
  • Equipment must be regularly inspected and meet industry standards
  • HIPAA compliance is mandatory for all patient data handling

5.2 Safety Requirements:

  • Current insurance coverage minimum $10M liability
  • FAA Part 135 certification or equivalent
  • Pilots must meet 14 CFR Part 135 experience requirements
  • Regular safety audits and compliance reporting
6. CUSTOMER PROTECTION AND PRIVACY

6.1 Provider Name Blurring:

To ensure fair competition and prevent direct customer contact bypassing the platform, Provider names will be displayed as "Provider A****", "Provider B****", etc. until booking confirmation. This protects both customer choice and platform integrity.

6.2 Data Protection:

  • All patient data is transient and automatically purged after 30 days
  • No credit card information is stored on SkyCareLink systems
  • Provider contact information is masked until booking confirmation
  • Session data is encrypted and HIPAA-compliant
7. PARTNERSHIP BENEFITS AND SUPPORT

7.1 Marketing Support:

  • Inclusion in SkyCareLink partner directory and promotional materials
  • Access to customer reviews and feedback systems
  • Co-marketing opportunities for qualified partners

7.2 Technology Platform:

  • Access to SkyCareLink partner dashboard for booking management
  • Real-time notification system for new booking requests
  • Performance analytics and revenue reporting tools
  • Customer communication tools and family update systems
8. TERMINATION AND MODIFICATION

Either party may terminate this agreement with 30 days written notice. Upon termination, all pending bookings will be completed under existing terms, and final payments will be processed within 7 business days.

This MOU may be modified only by written agreement signed by both parties. Any changes to revenue sharing, quality standards, or partnership benefits require 60 days advance notice.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any disputes arising under this agreement will be resolved through binding arbitration in Miami, Florida, under the rules of the American Arbitration Association. Both parties agree to attempt good-faith mediation before proceeding to arbitration.
